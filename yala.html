Yala National Park

About Yala National Park

Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka. Located in the southeastern region of the country, it is renowned for its rich biodiversity, especially the high density of leopards.

Highlights

  • Spotting elusive leopards in their natural habitat
  • Large elephant herds and various bird species
  • Spectacular coastal scenery and dunes
  • Safari rides through forests, lagoons, and scrubland

Tips for Visitors

The best time to visit Yala is from February to July when water levels are low, making wildlife easier to spot. Morning safaris offer better chances for leopard sightings.

Location

Yala is located in the southeastern part of Sri Lanka, about 300 km from Colombo.
