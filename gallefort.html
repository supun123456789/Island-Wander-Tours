Galle Fort

About Galle Fort

Galle Fort is a historic walled city on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka. Originally built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later fortified by the Dutch, it is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its well-preserved colonial architecture, cobbled streets, and seaside charm.

Highlights

  • Galle Lighthouse and ramparts
  • Dutch Reformed Church and colonial buildings
  • Charming cafés, galleries, and boutiques
  • Museum of Maritime Archaeology

Tips for Visitors

Explore Galle Fort on foot to enjoy the narrow lanes, ocean views, and hidden gems. The best time to visit is in the late afternoon for a sunset walk along the walls. Stay overnight to enjoy the ambiance after the day crowds leave.

Location

Galle Fort is located in the city of Galle, about 120 km south of Colombo, along Sri Lanka’s southwestern coast.
